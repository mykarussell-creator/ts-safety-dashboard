Official Incident Tier Definitions (T0–T8)
T0Squatter Interaction / Injury caused by bad actor / Weapon brandished
Highest severity. Triggers immediate investigation, Securitas dispatch, and TOM.
T1Injury in home
Customer, agent, or vendor physically injured on property. Legal/insurance escalation required.
T2Move-ins / Rental fraud occupancy
Victim moves in believing they are renting. Managed via JIRA. Resolution: NMO, C4K, or Eviction.
T3Drugs / Paraphernalia / Vandalism / Theft / Fire
Reported by customer or agent. Higher friction from reporting source vs T4/T5.
T4Drugs / Paraphernalia / Vandalism / Theft / Fire / Negative Interaction
Reported by OD employee, vendor, or neighbor.
T5Drugs / Paraphernalia / Vandalism / Theft / Squatter
Reported by Securitas or Law Enforcement. Often from patrol inspections or alarm responses.
T6Items left behind / Trespassing / Tampering
Lower severity physical evidence. SLA: 48 hrs to assignment, 48 hrs to done.
T7Fraud
Non-occupancy fraud events. SLA: 48 hrs to assignment, 48 hrs to done.
T8Toilet use / Other
Lowest severity. SLA: 48 hrs to assignment, 48 hrs to done.
Note: T3, T4, and T5 share the same incident typologies — the tier is determined by who reported it, not what happened. Source: Incident Tiers internal doc, last updated Feb 2025.
